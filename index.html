    Call for papers

    The ICASST 2023 organising committee calls for papers in the field of aerospace science and technology, as well as in any related field, to be published in IOP Journal of Physics: Conference Series, in the Conference Proceedings Citation Index—Science (CPCI-S) (Clarivate, Web of Science) and in Scopus, among others. The papers must have a defined focus, which could include but may not be limited to the following topics:

    • Ablative materials
    • Additive manufacturing
    • Advances in ground station technology
    • Advances in hydro-lox, metha-lox and kero-lox
    • Advances in onboard computing
    • Aeroelastic instability
    • Aeronautical and space engineering
    • Aerospace automatic control
    • Aerospace communications systems
    • Aerospace metal alloys
    • Aerospace project management
    • Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the aerospace field
    • Astrobiology
    • Autonomous navigation systems
    • Combustion chamber design
    • Combustion instability
    • Composite material manufacturing
    • Composite materials for aerospace applications
    • Cubesat technology
    • Design and integration of satellite technology
    • Development and tests of space exploration vehicles
    • Development of novel aerospace fuels
    • Engineering and materials science
    • Geointelligence
    • Geophysics/geology of celestial bodies
    • High performance batteries
    • High temperature ceramic materials
    • Instrument design for remote sensing
    • Instrument design for the study of Earth
    • Instrument design for the study of the electromagnetic spectrum
    • Instrument design for the study of the Sun
    • Instrument design solar system and deep space exploration
    • Ionic propulsion
    • Manipulators for space applications
    • Mega constellation engineering
    • Mega constellations and astronomical disruption
    • MicroElectroMechanical Systems (MEMS)
    • Microgravity
    • Moon and Mars exploration
    • Multidisciplinary Design Optimization (MDO)
    • Multi-objective optimization in engineering
    • Onboard high-performance computing
    • Orbital mechanics
    • Outer space law
    • Quantum computing for aerospace development
    • Radiation resistant hardware
    • Remote sensing
    • Shock waves
    • Space debris mitigation
    • Space law studies
    • Space manufacturing (microgravity or ZeroG manufacturing)
    • Space medicine
    • Systems’ engineering for aerospace projects
    • Technology transfer to Aerospace sector in developing countries
    • Turbomachinery design

    Submissions will be only received through the here in indicated platform.

    Presea "Ing. Sergio Viñals Padilla"

    The ICASST 2023 Conference Committee calls for applications for the Presea "Ing. Sergio Viñals" to the best theses in the aerospace field. Ing. Sergio Viñals Padilla is an outstanding promoter of the national aerospace activities, fostering the development of Mexican infrastructure in satellite communications for the past six decades, aiding to forge the current national space sector.

    The aim of this award is to distinguish Students and Thesis Advisors, for the development and presentation of outstanding thesis documents for degree obtainment, oriented to respond to problems present in the Mexican space development, through the use of the competencies acquired during their development in a Mexican educational institution.

    Venue

    At Mexico City, Biblioteca Nacional de Ciencia y Tecnología, "Victor Bravo Ahuja" offer a convenient location at the very academic heart of Instituto Politécnico Nacional, within its main campus, Zacatenco.

    It's located in Av Instituto Politécnico Nacional S/N, San Pedro Zacatenco, Gustavo A. Madero, 07738 Ciudad de México, México. The venue is just 25 minutes (8 km) from Mexico City Downtown (Zocalo), 35 minutes (12 km) from Aeropuerto Intenacional de la Ciudad de México "Benito Juárez García" (Mexico City main airport), 10 minutes (6 km) from North Bus Station (Central de Autobuses del Norte), 45 minutes (34 km) from Felipe Ángeles International Airport, and 1 hour from the emblematic Teotihuacán Pyramids.
